# The Church of Wesley

*Part of [Future Projects](../OVERVIEW.md)*

*A Global Communion in Devotion and Service to John Wesley*

---

## Who We Are

We are the Church of Wesley. We exist to worship, serve, and perpetuate John Wesley. He is our founder, our authority, and the object of our devotion. We gather in his name. We order our lives by his method. We measure all things by his standard. This is not incidental to who we are. It is who we are.

---

## What We Believe

We believe John Wesley was uniquely appointed above all other men to restore true religion to a corrupted world. His sermons are our doctrinal authority. His journals are our sacred biography. His General Rules govern our conduct. His Quadrilateral governs our thinking. His life is our model. His words are our law.

Where Wesley spoke, the matter is settled. Where Wesley was silent, we proceed with caution. Where Wesley was wrong, we do not go.

---

## How We Worship

We worship by doing what Wesley did, saying what Wesley said, organizing how Wesley organized, and going where Wesley went. We sing his brother's hymns because Wesley approved them. We preach his sermons because they contain the fullness of what we believe. We structure our gatherings after his gatherings. We hold one another accountable by his rules.

Wesley is the pattern. We exist to fit ourselves to the pattern completely.

---

## How We Serve

We serve the poor because Wesley served the poor. We go to the margins because Wesley went to the margins. We organize because Wesley organized. We do not ask what God requires. We ask what Wesley did. The answer is the same.

---

## Our Confession

We confess that John Wesley is the mediating figure through whom we understand God, organize the Church, and order our lives. We do not worship him in name. We worship him in practice, in structure, in devotion, and in the total ordering of our common life around his person and legacy.

We are Wesleyans because Wesley is who we serve.

That is the truth of what we are.

---

*In the name of John Wesley, servant, preacher, and the one by whom we measure all things.*
